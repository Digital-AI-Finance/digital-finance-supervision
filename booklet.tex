\goldsection{Foreword from the Organizers}

\vspace{4pt}
{\itshape\color{medgray}\large A letter from the co-chairs}
\vspace{10pt}

\begin{spacing}{1.15}
Dear colleagues and friends,

It is our great pleasure to welcome you to the \textbf{Digital Finance for Supervision} workshop, hosted at the \textbf{European Central Bank} in Frankfurt am Main on December 3, 2026.

This workshop represents a unique convergence of academic research and supervisory practice. As financial supervision enters a transformative era driven by artificial intelligence, natural language processing, and large language models, there is a pressing need to bridge the gap between cutting-edge research and real-world supervisory applications.

The \textbf{DIGITAL network} --- funded by the European Union's Horizon Europe programme under the Marie Sk\l{}odowska-Curie Actions --- brings together 22 partner institutions from across Europe, including universities, central banks, financial institutions, and technology companies. Our shared mission is to advance the frontiers of digital finance research while training the next generation of researchers who can navigate both the technical and regulatory dimensions of financial innovation.

Today's workshop focuses specifically on the intersection of digital technologies and banking supervision. We have assembled a programme that features keynote addresses from leading experts, research presentations on AI/ML for bank supervision, NLP applications for regulatory reporting, and the emerging use of large language models in supervisory processes.

We are particularly grateful to the ECB for hosting this event and for the active engagement of ECB staff in both the organization and the intellectual programme. The collaboration between the ECB and the DIGITAL network exemplifies the kind of partnership between supervisory authorities and academic institutions that is essential for responsible innovation in financial supervision.

We encourage you to engage actively in the discussions, connect with fellow researchers and practitioners, and explore opportunities for collaboration. The challenges of digital supervision are too significant for any single institution to address alone --- together, we can shape a supervisory framework that is both technologically advanced and robustly grounded.

Thank you for joining us. We look forward to a stimulating and productive day.
\end{spacing}

\goldsection{About the Workshop}

\vspace{2pt}
The \textbf{Digital Finance for Supervision} workshop brings together researchers from the MSCA DIGITAL network and supervision practitioners from the European Central Bank to explore how artificial intelligence, natural language processing, and large language models are reshaping financial supervision across Europe.

This one-day event features keynote presentations, research sessions, and panel discussions designed to foster dialogue between academia and supervisory practice. Participants will explore state-of-the-art research on supervisory technology (SupTech) and its implications for the future of banking oversight.

\goldsection{Research Topics}

\vspace{4pt}
{\color{medgray} The workshop focuses on three interconnected research themes at the frontier of supervisory technology.}
\vspace{10pt}

\topiccard{1 \quad AI/ML for Bank Supervision}{%
Artificial intelligence and machine learning are transforming how supervisory authorities assess bank risk, detect anomalies, and allocate inspection resources. This track explores the application of supervised and unsupervised learning methods to supervisory datasets, stress testing, early warning systems, and risk classification.}{%
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=12pt, itemsep=1pt, label={\color{ecbgold}\footnotesize$\blacksquare$}]
  \item Predictive models for bank distress and early warning indicators
  \item ML-based anomaly detection in supervisory reporting data
  \item AI-driven stress testing and scenario analysis
  \item Explainability and interpretability in supervisory AI models
\end{itemize}%
}

\vspace{10pt}

\topiccard{2 \quad NLP for Regulatory Reporting}{%
Natural language processing offers powerful tools for extracting structured information from the vast volumes of unstructured text that supervisory authorities process daily. This track examines how NLP techniques can automate and enhance regulatory reporting, compliance checking, and document analysis.}{%
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=12pt, itemsep=1pt, label={\color{ecbgold}\footnotesize$\blacksquare$}]
  \item Automated analysis of regulatory filings and annual reports
  \item Sentiment analysis for supervisory intelligence
  \item Named entity recognition in financial documents
  \item Text classification for compliance and risk assessment
\end{itemize}%
}

\vspace{10pt}

\topiccard{3 \quad LLMs in Supervision}{%
Large language models represent the latest frontier in AI for financial supervision. This track investigates how foundation models and generative AI can support supervisory workflows, from drafting supervisory assessments to answering complex regulatory queries and summarizing extensive documentation.}{%
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=12pt, itemsep=1pt, label={\color{ecbgold}\footnotesize$\blacksquare$}]
  \item LLM-assisted supervisory report generation
  \item Retrieval-augmented generation for regulatory Q\&A
  \item Fine-tuning foundation models on supervisory corpora
  \item Governance, bias, and hallucination risks in supervisory LLMs
\end{itemize}%
}
